Civic Dictionary

Master every term in the democratic process.

search

A

Absentee Ballot

A ballot submitted before election day, typically by mail, for voters who cannot be present at their designated polling place.

Amendment

A formal change or addition to the Constitution or a law, proposed and ratified through a specific legal process.

Apportionment

The process of dividing the 435 seats in the U.S. House of Representatives among the 50 states based on population data from the census.

B

Ballot

The official document or electronic interface used by a voter to make their choices in an election.

Ballot Measure

A proposed law or amendment placed on the ballot for voters to approve or reject directly.

Bipartisan

Involving or supported by two major political parties, often to show cross-party agreement.

C

Caucus

A meeting of members of a political party, especially to select delegates to a convention or to decide on policy.

Census

The official count of the population conducted every 10 years; used for Congressional apportionment and redistricting.

Civic Duty

The responsibilities of a citizen, including voting, jury duty, and staying informed about government.

Constituency

The body of voters in a specific area who elect a representative to a legislative body.

D

Delegate

A person authorized to represent others, particularly at a political convention to vote for a candidate.

Democracy

A system of government where citizens exercise power by voting, either directly or through elected representatives.

E

Electoral College

A body of 538 electors who formally cast votes to elect the President and Vice President of the United States.

Electorate

All the people in a country or area who are entitled to vote in an election.

F

Filibuster

A tactic used in the U.S. Senate to delay or block a vote by extending debate on a proposal.

Franchise

The right to vote in public elections; also known as suffrage.

G

Gerrymandering

The manipulation of electoral district boundaries to favor a particular party or group.

General Election

The final election in which voters choose among candidates nominated in primary elections or by parties.

I

Inauguration

The formal ceremony of swearing in a newly elected president and the beginning of their term.

Incumbent

The person currently holding an official position, who may be running for re-election.

P

Primary Election

An election held to narrow the field of candidates before the general election. Voters choose their party's nominee.

Polling Place

The designated location where voters go to cast their ballots on election day.

R

Redistricting

The process of redrawing electoral district boundaries, usually after the census, to reflect population changes.

Referendum

A direct vote in which an entire electorate is asked to either accept or reject a particular proposal.

S

Suffrage

The right to vote in political elections; also known as franchise.

Swing State

A state where neither party has a dominant majority, making it competitive in elections.

V

Voter Registration

The process by which eligible citizens sign up to vote, providing information to election officials.

Voter Turnout

The percentage of eligible voters who cast a ballot in an election.
