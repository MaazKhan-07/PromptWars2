Election Glossary

Democracy shouldn't be a puzzle. Explore our comprehensive database of election terms defined in plain English.

search

A

Absentee Ballot

A ballot cast by a voter who is unable to go to their polling place on Election Day.

Adjudication

The process of determining the intent of a voter when a ballot cannot be read by a machine.

B

Ballot

A device used to cast votes in an election, usually a piece of paper or a digital interface.

Bipartisan

Supported by two political parties that usually oppose each other's policies.

C

Canvass

The aggregate of all ballots cast in an election to verify result accuracy.

Caucus

A meeting of supporters or members of a specific political party or movement.

M

Midterm Election

Elections held in the middle of a presidential term for congressional and state seats.

E

Electoral Vote

The vote cast in the Electoral College by representatives of each state to determine the President.

lightbulb

A candidate needs 270 electoral votes—a simple majority of the 538 total—to win.

G

Gerrymandering

The practice of manipulating the boundaries of an electoral constituency so as to favor one party.

P

Plurality

The number of votes cast for a candidate who receives more than any other but does not receive an absolute majority.

Primary Election

An election to appoint delegates to a party conference or to select candidates for a principal election.

V

Voter Suppression

A strategy used to influence the outcome of an election by discouraging or preventing specific groups of people from voting.
