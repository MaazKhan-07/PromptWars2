Election Journey

A comprehensive guide through the democratic process. Understand every milestone from filing to the inauguration.

01 edit_note

Candidacy Filing

Official declaration of intent to run for office, meeting legal requirements and deadlines.

Candidates must submit formal paperwork, signatures, and pay filing fees according to local or state laws. This stage ensures only qualified individuals appear on the ballot.
Learn More arrow_forward
02 groups

Primaries

Party members vote to select their preferred candidate for the general election phase.

Primaries can be 'open' (any registered voter) or 'closed' (only party members). Winners represent their party in the General Election.
Learn More arrow_forward
03 podium

Debates

Candidates face off to discuss policies, vision, and respond to public concerns on live air.

Debates provide a platform for voters to compare candidates' stances side-by-side on critical issues like economy, health, and security.
Learn More arrow_forward
04 event_available

Registration Deadlines

The final dates for citizens to register to vote in the upcoming general election cycle.

Deadlines vary by state. Many states offer online registration, while some allow same-day registration at polling places.
Learn More arrow_forward
05 how_to_vote

Early Voting

Opportunity for voters to cast their ballots before the official election day at designated sites.

Early voting helps reduce lines on Election Day and provides flexibility for citizens with difficult work schedules or travel plans.
Learn More arrow_forward
06 flag

Election Day

The primary day for nationwide voting. Polls open across all districts and time zones.

Voters typically cast ballots at their assigned local polling stations. Absentee and mail-in ballots must usually be received by this day.
Learn More arrow_forward
07 calculate

Counting

Secure tallying of all cast ballots, including absentee and provisional votes, by election officials.

Counting is often observed by representatives of both parties to ensure transparency. Unofficial results are usually broadcast on election night.
Learn More arrow_forward
08 verified

Certification

Formal process where election results are verified and legally finalized by governing boards.

Once all votes are tallied and any challenges are resolved, officials formally certify the winner of each race.
Learn More arrow_forward
09 account_balance

Electoral College Vote

Electors meet in their respective states to cast their official votes for President and Vice President.

In the U.S., the President is technically elected by electors rather than the direct popular vote. A candidate needs 270 votes to win.
Learn More arrow_forward
10 celebration

Inauguration

The formal ceremony marking the beginning of a new term for the elected official.

The winner takes the oath of office, affirming their commitment to uphold the laws and serve the citizens of their jurisdiction.
